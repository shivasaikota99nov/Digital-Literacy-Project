# The Digital Double-Edge: Navigating the Rise of UPI Fraud
The rapid expansion of the Unified Payments Interface (UPI) in India—reaching over 21 billion monthly transactions by early 2026—has created a parallel surge in sophisticated digital payment fraud. This case study examines the evolution of these scams and the regulatory response.

The "Collect Request" & Social Engineering Scam.

In a typical 2026 scenario, a fraudster poses as a buyer on a secondary marketplace (like OLX). Instead of sending money to the seller, they initiate a "Collect Request" on a UPI app. They use social engineering to convince the victim that "entering your PIN is necessary to authorize the receipt of funds." Because UPI is designed for instant settlement, the moment the victim enters their PIN, the funds are irrevocably debited.

Other emerging 2026 trends include:

 1. QR Code Tampering: Replacing legitimate merchant QRs with fraudulent ones.

 2. Remote Access Scams: Using screen-sharing apps to capture PINs during "fake customer support" calls.

 3. SIM-Swap Fraud: Hijacking mobile identities to bypass SMS-based authentication.
